The transition from the metastable inflationary plateau to the radiation-dominated era is currently modeled as a sharp "quantum breaking" event at $t_{qb}$. I hypothesize that the transition is not instantaneous but characterized by a **non-adiabatic particle production phase** driven by the rapid decay of the Bogoliubov modes as the condensate reaches its holographic capacity. Specifically, I propose that the coupling $\gamma$ is not a constant but a dynamical function $\gamma(N, Q_{mem})$ that tracks the "information density" of the condensate. By modeling the transition as a **phase-space trajectory shift** where the system moves from a stable node to a chaotic regime as $Q_{mem} \to N$, we can derive a time-dependent reheating temperature $T_{rh}$ that is intrinsically linked to the species count $N_s$. This hypothesis will be tested by simulating the evolution of the system beyond the $Q_{mem} \approx N$ threshold to determine if the "graceful exit" produces a thermal spectrum of particles consistent with the Big Bang, thereby replacing the ad-hoc reheating potential with a purely information-theoretic decay process.
