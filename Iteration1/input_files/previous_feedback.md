The current analysis successfully establishes the graviton condensate as a self-regulating dynamical system. The derivation of the stability of the inflationary plateau and the identification of the $F(x) \sim \sqrt{x}$ scaling as a potential "attractor" for the observed inflationary scale are significant steps forward. However, to move from a theoretical model to a robust scientific framework, the following critical gaps must be addressed:

**1. Justification of the Memory Load Functional $F(x)$:**
The selection of $F(x) = \sqrt{M_{Pl}/H}$ is currently an empirical fit to match the observed $H$. This is a major weakness. You must provide a physical derivation or a stronger theoretical motivation for why the memory load should scale as the square root of the inverse Hubble parameter. Is this related to the density of states of the Bogoliubov modes or the geometry of the horizon? Without a microscopic derivation, this remains a "fine-tuning" of the functional form rather than a dynamical selection.

**2. The "Graceful Exit" Problem:**
You claim the system has a "natural, built-in graceful exit." However, the current dynamical system shows the system settling into a stable attractor ($dN/dt = 0$). If the system is a stable node or spiral, it will stay there indefinitely until $t_{qb}$ is reached. You need to explicitly model the transition at $t_{qb}$. Does the system "crash" (quantum break) abruptly, or does the feedback mechanism change as $Q_{mem} \to N$? You must show how the transition from the metastable plateau to the end of inflation occurs without requiring an external trigger.

**3. Sensitivity to the Coupling Parameter $\gamma$:**
The stability analysis shows that the nature of the attractor (node vs. spiral) depends on $\gamma$. You have treated $\gamma$ as a constant. In a quantum-N framework, $\gamma$ (related to the graviton coupling $\alpha$) should evolve as the condensate changes. You should perform a sensitivity analysis where $\gamma$ is a function of $N$ (e.g., $\gamma \sim 1/N$) to see if the stability of the attractor is preserved under more realistic coupling conditions.

**4. Addressing the "Species" Definition:**
You treat $N_s$ as a free parameter. In the context of the Standard Model or GUTs, $N_s$ is not a continuous variable. You should map the "Stability Corridor" specifically for discrete values of $N_s$ corresponding to known physics (e.g., $N_s \approx 10^2$ for SM, $N_s \approx 10^3-10^4$ for SUSY/GUTs). This will ground your abstract phase diagram in physical reality and clarify whether your model predicts a specific $H$ for the Standard Model.

**5. Missed Opportunity: Tensor-to-Scalar Ratio ($r$):**
Since you are working in the inflationary epoch, you have the opportunity to link your model to observables. The Hubble scale $H$ is directly related to the tensor-to-scalar ratio $r \propto H^2$. Use your derived $H$ values to predict a range for $r$. If your model predicts $r$ values that are already excluded by BICEP/Keck or Planck, you have a powerful way to constrain your $F(x)$ functional forms.
