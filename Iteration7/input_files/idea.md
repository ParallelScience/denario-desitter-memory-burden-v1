**Title**: Dynamical Stability and Information-Theoretic Constraints of the Graviton Condensate Inflationary Phase

This research establishes the inflationary epoch as a metastable graviton condensate where the Hubble scale $H$ emerges from the feedback equilibrium $dN/dt = -H + \gamma Q_{mem}/N^2 = 0$. By performing a linear stability analysis of this fixed point, we demonstrate that the memory burden $Q_{mem}$ acts as a self-regulating pressure, ensuring the condensate remains at quantum criticality ($Q_{mem} \approx N$) without a fundamental cosmological constant. We derive the species-entropy constraint $N_s \leq M_{Pl}^2/H^2$ and integrate the quantum breaking time $t_{qb}$ to prove the relation $N_e \cdot N_s \leq M_{Pl}^2/H^2$, which strictly bounds the inflationary duration relative to the microscopic species count. To resolve the dynamical selection of $H$, we conduct a sensitivity analysis on the memory load functional $F(M_{Pl}/H)$ across varying power-law exponents to identify the specific conditions under which the system is driven toward the observed inflationary scale $10^{-5} \leq H/M_{Pl} \leq 10^{-4}$. We will produce phase diagrams in $(N_s, H/M_{Pl})$ space to map the stability corridors where the memory burden prevents quantum breaking, effectively constraining the allowed inflationary parameter space by the maximum information storage capacity of the de Sitter horizon. This analysis provides a rigorous, self-consistent framework for inflation that replaces the inflaton potential with the dynamical saturation of gravito-information modes.
