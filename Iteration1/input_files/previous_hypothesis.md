The "graceful exit" from the inflationary phase is not merely a cessation of the metastable state, but a phase transition triggered by the saturation of the memory capacity $Q_{mem} \approx N$. We hypothesize that the transition from a stable node to a stable spiral in the dynamical system $dN/dt = -H + \gamma Q_{mem}/N^2$ represents the onset of "quantum breaking," where the backreaction of the memory modes becomes non-linear and dissipative. By modeling the coupling parameter $\gamma$ as a dynamical variable $\gamma(N, Q_{mem})$ that accounts for the backreaction of the Bogoliubov modes on the condensate geometry, we can derive a "critical slowing down" signature in the Hubble parameter $H(t)$ as $t \to t_{qb}$. We propose that the observed spectral index $n_s$ of the primordial power spectrum is a direct consequence of the time-dependent evolution of the memory load $Q_{mem}(t)$ during this final approach to the quantum breaking threshold. This hypothesis will be tested by numerically solving the coupled system for $H(t)$ and $Q_{mem}(t)$ to determine if the transition to the spiral regime naturally produces a deviation from scale-invariance consistent with the observed $n_s \approx 0.96$, thereby linking the microscopic memory burden to the macroscopic CMB observables without requiring an inflaton potential.
