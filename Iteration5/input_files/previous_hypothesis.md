The "graceful exit" from inflation is currently modeled as a threshold-triggered event at $t_{qb}$ where the condensate reaches saturation. I hypothesize that the transition is not a sharp cutoff but a **stochastic phase transition driven by the backreaction of the Bogoliubov mode spectrum's infrared (IR) divergence**. Specifically, as $Q_{mem} \to N$, the energy density of the memory modes $\epsilon_k \sim 1/N$ undergoes a "condensation-to-thermalization" transition, where the memory modes cease to be coherent information channels and instead act as a thermal bath of gravitons. 

I propose to modify the dynamical system to include a **stochastic noise term** in the $dN/dt$ equation, representing the fluctuations in the memory accumulation rate $\delta(dQ_{mem}/dt) \sim \sqrt{N_s} H$. By simulating the system near the saturation limit $Q_{mem} \approx N$ with this noise, we can determine if the "graceful exit" is a deterministic collapse or a stochastic tunneling event. This will allow us to calculate the **probability distribution of the e-folding number $N_e$** rather than a single value, providing a theoretical prediction for the variance in the duration of inflation. This approach will test whether the observed spectral index $n_s$ is sensitive to the "noise" of the quantum breaking process, potentially linking the microscopic species count $N_s$ to the observed scalar tilt $n_s$ through the statistics of the exit transition.
