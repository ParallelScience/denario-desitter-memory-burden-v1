The transition from the inflationary plateau to the radiation-dominated era is driven by the "Species-Induced Phase Transition" (SIPT) of the graviton condensate. I hypothesize that as the condensate approaches the quantum breaking time $t_{qb}$, the effective number of species $N_s$ is not constant but evolves as a function of the decreasing Hubble scale $H(t)$, specifically $N_s(H) \propto (M_{Pl}/H)^\beta$. By coupling the dynamical system $dN/dt$ and $dQ_{mem}/dt$ with a time-dependent $N_s(H)$ that accounts for the sequential decoupling of particle species as the energy scale drops, we can demonstrate that the "graceful exit" is a self-triggered instability. This hypothesis posits that the system undergoes a bifurcation when $N_s(H)$ crosses the threshold $S_{dS}$, causing the memory burden $Q_{mem}$ to decouple from the condensate stability, leading to a rapid collapse of the quasi-de Sitter phase into a hot Big Bang state. This approach will replace the ad-hoc $\tanh$-based transition function with a physically motivated, state-dependent feedback loop, providing a unified mechanism for both the duration of inflation and the reheating process.
